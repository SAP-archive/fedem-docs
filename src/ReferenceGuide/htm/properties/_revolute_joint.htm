Revolute Joint

The Revolute joint object is used to represent a Revolute joint in a FEDEM model. The Revolute joint has one degree of freedom (DOF) that allows rotation of one Part with respect to another Part, about a given axis (the common Z-axis). Optionally, it may also have the freedom to translate one Part with respect to the other Part along the common Z-axis.

Each Revolute joint uses two Triads to connect the joint to Parts. One of the Triads is labeled master while the other one is labeled slave, with the constrained DOFs of the slave triad following the movement of the master triad. See the FEDEM Theory Guide, Chapter 6 "Modeling of Joints". To determine which Triad is the master and which is the slave, select one of the Parts or the Revolute joint itself to examine the Topology panel of master and slave triads connected to the Part or Revolute joint. You can then select (click and hold down the mouse button) the master- or slave triad to highlight it in the Modeller window. You may also interchange the master and slave triads in a Revolute joint (unless its master triad also is a slave triad in another joint), by by clicking the Swap Master and Slave Triad button located underneath the ID and Topology panel.

See the section on "Joints" in the "Mechanism Elements" chapter in the FEDEM User's Guide, for more information on Revolute joints.
