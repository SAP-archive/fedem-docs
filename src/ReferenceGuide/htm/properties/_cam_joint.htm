Cam Joint

The Cam joint object is used to represent a Cam joint in a FEDEM model. A Cam joint has one (or two) spring-damper constrained DOFs, and five (or four) unconstrained DOFs that allow the slave triad (called the follower) to move over a curved surface (or curve), referred to as the cam surface. The cam surface is defined by a curve consisting of circular arc segment. Each arc segment is defined by the location of three master triads. A Cam joint must consist of one slave/follower triad and at least three master triads. See also the FEDEM Theory Guide, Section 6.3.3 "Cam joint."

See the section on "Joints" in the "Mechanism Elements" chapter in the FEDEM User's Guide, for more information on Cam joints.
