Ball Joint

The Ball joint object is used to represent a Ball joint in a FEDEM model. The Ball joint has three degrees of freedom (DOFs) that allow rotation of one Part with respect to another Part about three axes.

Each Ball joint uses two Triads to connect the joint to Parts. One of the Triads is labeled master while the other one is labeled slave, with the constrained DOFs of the slave triad following the movement of the master triad. The three DOFs of the Ball joint are defined by the angles between the master triad and the slave triad in the x-, y-, and z-directions. See the FEDEM Theory Guide, Chapter 6 "Modeling of Joints". To determine which Triad is the master and which is the slave, select one of the Parts or the Ball joint itself to examine the Topology panel of master and slave triads connected to the Part or Ball joint. You can then select (click and hold down the mouse button) the master- or slave triad to highlight it in the Modeller window. You may also interchange the master and slave triads in a Ball joint (unless its master triad also is a slave triad in another joint), by by clicking the Swap Master and Slave Triad button located underneath the ID and Topology panel.

See the section on "Joints" in the "Mechanism Elements" chapter in the FEDEM User's Guide, for more information on Ball joints.
