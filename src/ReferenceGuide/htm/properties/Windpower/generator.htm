Generator

The Generator object is a sub-assembly used to represent the electrical generator in a wind turbine model.

The figure above illustrates the Generator properties, which consist of the following items:

Name:Description:
MassDisplays the total mass of the generator.
Center of GravityDisplays the location of the generator's center of gravity, in either Global coordinates or in Local coordinates with respect to the origin of the generator assembly.
Torque controlSpecifies a constant value or a function that controls the applied torque.
Velocity controlSpecifies a constant value or a function that controls the prescribed velocity.

The Generator sub-assembly normally contains one Revolute Joint and one Triad in its model tree list. The Revolute joint is automatically assigned in its "Rz"-tab, the properties you specify as Torque control or Velocity control, and changing the values in the joint property will similarly automatically update the Generator properties. You can model advanced generator behaviour by using a control system to produce the applied torque or prescribed velocity. Please see the chapter on "Wind Power Modelling" in the FEDEM User's Guide for more information on such modelling.
