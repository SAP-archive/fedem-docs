Damper

The Damper object is used to introduce discrete damping in a FEDEM model. There are two types of dampers in FEDEM: Axial- and joint dampers. Axial dampers apply relative forces between two Triads along the direction axis between the triads, whereas Joint dampers are integrated in the Joint, and act on the joint triads along one of the unconstrained DOFs of the Joint.

The figure above illustrates the Damper properties, which consist of the following items:

Name:Description:
Damping coefficientIn this field either a constant damping coefficient can be entered, or you can select a defined damper characteristic from the pull-down list.
Scale The damper force can be scaled by a function. This can for instance be used to switch the damper on and off during the simulation. When no function is selected the scale is set to 1.0. The Scale can not be a function of the damper velocity or length.
Use deformational velocity This option is available if the damper is acting together with a spring with a forced change in its stress free length. The option enables the usage of the deformational velocity of the connected spring, when evaluating the damper. The deformational velocity is the spring velocity without the velocity component coming from a forced change in the stress free length.
